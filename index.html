Intro

Get to know us more

The Joseph Alabi Adewoye Foundation is more than just an foundation — it’s a living tribute to a man whose legacy continues to inspire generations. We are driven by a deep desire to empower the people of Ijara-Isin and surrounding communities in Kwara State through initiatives that promote education, human capacity development, environmental sustainability, and cultural preservation.

Join us on this journey as we celebrate the past, embrace the present, and shape a better future for our communities.

Who We Are

About The Foundation

The Joseph Alabi Adewoye Foundation is a legally registered non-governmental organization (RC 8401335) established to honor the legacy of Mr. Joseph Alabi Adewoye, a visionary artist, educator, and community advocate. Rooted in Ijara-Isin, Kwara State, the foundation is dedicated to advancing human and environmental development within the region and beyond.

Our organization focuses on initiatives that reflect the values and contributions of Mr. Adewoye — from education and cultural promotion to sustainable development and youth empowerment. We believe in building communities through creativity, knowledge, and compassion.

Governed by a dedicated Board of Trustees and supported by passionate individuals, the foundation aims to create long-lasting impact that aligns with Mr. Adewoye’s principles and passions.

Journey

Biography – Mr. Joseph Alabi Adewoye

Joseph Alabi Adewoye, B.A. Fine Arts (Ahmadu Bello University), Cert. S.P.P (London), was a pioneer graphic artist and educator. Born in Ijara, Kwara State, he began his educational journey at Provincial Secondary School, Ilorin (1959), and later attended Bauchi Teachers College. He graduated from Ahmadu Bello University in 1965 with a degree in Fine Arts.

After graduation, he worked as a Graphic Art Officer for the Northern Nigeria Ministry of Information and later headed the Graphic Art Section at the Kwara State Military Governor’s Office in 1968. He furthered his education in London at the London College of Printing, specializing in screen printing, airbrushing, typography, and package design.

Upon returning to Nigeria in 1969, he continued his contributions to the arts and public service. In 1973, he was appointed Lecturer in Graphic Design at Ahmadu Bello University. Mr. Adewoye also represented Kwara State in numerous cultural and national events, serving on the Niger Art Council and the National Festival Committee.
